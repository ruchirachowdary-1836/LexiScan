MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 15, 2024,
by and between Acme Corporation, Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Client"), and
Vendor LLC, a California limited liability company ("Vendor"), collectively referred
to as the "Parties."

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Client desires to obtain certain software development and consulting services;
WHEREAS, Vendor has the expertise and capability to provide such services;
NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein, the Parties agree:

1. SERVICES

1.1 Scope of Services. Vendor agrees to provide software development, technical consulting,
and related services (the "Services") as described in Statements of Work ("SOW") executed
by both Parties. Each SOW shall specify deliverables, timelines, and compensation.

1.2 Standard of Performance. Vendor shall perform all Services in a professional and
workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards. Vendor shall assign qualified
personnel to perform the Services.

2. PAYMENT TERMS

2.1 Fees. Client shall pay Vendor the fees specified in each SOW within thirty (30) days
of receipt of invoice. All amounts are in United States Dollars (USD).

2.2 Late Payment. Payments not received within the due date shall accrue interest at
1.5% per month (18% per annum) from the due date until paid.

2.3 Expenses. Client shall reimburse Vendor for all pre-approved, reasonable, and
documented out-of-pocket expenses incurred in performing the Services.

3. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

3.1 Work for Hire. All work product, deliverables, software, code, documentation, and
materials created by Vendor under this Agreement shall be considered "work made for hire"
as defined under the U.S. Copyright Act. Client shall own all intellectual property rights,
title, and interest in and to all deliverables.

3.2 Assignment. To the extent any deliverable does not qualify as work made for hire,
Vendor hereby irrevocably assigns to Client all rights, title, and interest therein,
including all patent, copyright, and trade secret rights.

3.3 Pre-Existing IP. Vendor retains ownership of any pre-existing intellectual property
("Background IP") incorporated into deliverables. Vendor grants Client a perpetual,
royalty-free license to use such Background IP solely in connection with the deliverables.

4. CONFIDENTIALITY

4.1 Obligations. Each Party agrees to maintain in strict confidence all Confidential
Information of the other Party and not to disclose such information to any third party
without prior written consent. "Confidential Information" means any non-public information
disclosed by one Party to the other in connection with this Agreement.

4.2 Exceptions. Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that: (a) is or
becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement; (b) was rightfully known before
disclosure; or (c) is required to be disclosed by law or court order.

5. INDEMNIFICATION

5.1 Vendor Indemnification. Vendor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client and
its officers, directors, employees, and agents (collectively, "Client Indemnitees") from
and against any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable
attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to: (a) Vendor's breach of this Agreement;
(b) Vendor's negligent or wrongful acts or omissions; or (c) any infringement of third-party
intellectual property rights by Vendor's deliverables.

6. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

6.1 NOTWITHSTANDING ANYTHING TO THE CONTRARY IN THIS AGREEMENT, VENDOR SHALL HAVE
ABSOLUTELY NO LIMITATION ON LIABILITY AND CLIENT MAY RECOVER UNLIMITED DAMAGES from Vendor
in the event of gross negligence, willful misconduct, fraud, or intentional breach.
VENDOR'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY TO CLIENT IS UNCAPPED AND UNLIMITED.

6.2 Exclusion of Consequential Damages. Except as set forth in Section 6.1, in no event
shall either Party be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, or consequential damages.

7. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

7.1 Non-Compete. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of two (2) years
following its termination, Vendor shall not, directly or indirectly, engage in, own,
manage, operate, or participate in any business that competes with Client's business
in any market in which Client operates or has operated during the preceding 24 months.

7.2 Non-Solicitation. For a period of two (2) years following termination, Vendor shall
not directly or indirectly solicit or hire any employee, contractor, or consultant of
Client who was involved in the Services during the term of this Agreement.

8. TERM AND TERMINATION

8.1 Term. This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for one (1) year
("Initial Term"), with automatic renewal for successive one-year terms unless either party
provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the end of the
then-current term.

8.2 Termination for Convenience. Either party may terminate this Agreement for any reason
or no reason upon thirty (30) days prior written notice to the other party.

8.3 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate immediately upon written notice if
the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within
fifteen (15) days of written notice.

9. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

9.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with
the laws of the State of Delaware, without giving effect to any choice of law provisions.

9.2 Arbitration. Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved
by binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association under its
Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted in Wilmington, Delaware.

10. FORCE MAJEURE

Neither party shall be liable for any delay or failure to perform its obligations under
this Agreement to the extent such delay or failure is caused by circumstances beyond its
reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, war,
terrorism, labor disputes, or government actions.

11. MISCELLANEOUS

11.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties
with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations and agreements.

11.2 Anti-Assignment. Neither Party may assign this Agreement or any rights hereunder
without the prior written consent of the other Party. Any purported assignment in violation
hereof shall be null and void.

11.3 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is found invalid or unenforceable,
the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.
